Eiji Aonuma

My favorite game Producer

Here's a time line of Aonume's life:

  • 1963 - Born in Nagano, Chubu
  • 1998 - Attended the Tokyo National University of Fine Arts and Music
  • 1994 - Worked as a Screen Designer for Super Game Boy
  • 1998 - Worked as a Game System Designer for the big title "The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time"
  • 2000 - Worked as a Director in the development of "The Legend of Zelda: Majora's Mask"
  • 2002 - Worked as a Director on "The Legend of Zelda: The Wind Waker"
  • 2003 - Served as a Producer on "The Legend of Zelda: Collector's Edition"
  • 2004 - Worked as a Producer on "The Legend of Zelda: Four Swords Adventures" and as a Supervisor on "The Legend of Zelda: The Minish Cap"
  • 2004 - Worked as a Producer on "The Legend of Zelda: Four Swords Adventures"
  • 2011 - Worked again on the "Ocarina of Time" title, but this time as a Producer on "The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time 3D"
  • 2017 - One of my favorite titles he has worked with and definitely the most exciting one was "The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild"

    • Fun fact:

    "To be honest with you, I just don't like action games that require you to jump. They're scary. The jumping factor kind of freaks me out."

    - Aonuma Eiji
